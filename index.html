The "Third Digital Divide" is a Knowledge Gap.

We know low-income students often lack access to hardware and broadband. But the "third digital divide" reveals a deeper issue: they lack human guidance to use technology effectively, beyond coding, but to lead and innovate.

The Talent Void

Globally, we face a shortage of 70 million teachers by 2030.

The "People Gap"

Affluent students have access to tutors and parents in STEM fields. Underserved students do not.

The Brain Drain

As the "founders" of the digital age retire, a massive reservoir of technical knowledge is walking out the door.

Where Legacy Meets Potential.

Our Mission

To bridge the digital and intergenerational divides by empowering students to adapt and shape future technology guided by the very experts who built the industry.

Our Vision

A world where young people have the skills to thrive in a future shaped by advanced technology, accelerated by the wisdom of the generation that started it all.

Core Values

Equity • Innovation • Lifelong Learning • Mastery

Our flagship program goes beyond basic computer literacy. It creates a transfer of power from one generation to the next.

The Curriculum

  • Immersive Tech: Hands-on experience with VR and Mixed Reality to explore STEM fields and immersive simulations.
  • AI Literacy: Students navigate the world of Artificial Intelligence, learning how to command the tools of tomorrow.

The Mentors (The "55+ Tech Squad")

This isn't just volunteering; it's a legacy project. Our mentors are adults 55+ with deep technical backgrounds.

  • They are the systems architects, early coders, and hardware engineers of the late 20th century.
  • They provide 1-on-1 guidance, translating complex concepts into graspable skills.
  • They don't just teach the "how"—they teach the "why," sharing the history and ethics of the tools students use today.

Our Impact

Measuring the Spark. Scaling the Flame.

We view impact through two lenses: the immediate, life-changing connections happening in our pilot classrooms today, and the systemic shift we are engineering for tomorrow.

1. Impact Now: The Pilot (Proof of Concept)

Currently active in Year 1 (Q1-Q2)

We are currently proving that the "Third Digital Divide" can be bridged by human connection. In our controlled pilot phase, we are laser-focused on depth of interaction rather than breadth.

The Cohort

We are serving 50 students across 2 low-income schools.

The Mentors

A squad of 10 Tech Pioneers—retired engineers and IT specialists—are actively mentoring, bringing decades of high-level industry experience directly to the students.

Early Wins

  • For Students: Measurable improvement in AI literacy and a marked increase in school attendance.
  • For Mentors: 100% of our pilot mentors report a renewed sense of purpose and a reduction in social isolation.

"I built servers for 30 years. Today, I watched a 12-year-old use AI to design a server farm in seconds. She taught me speed; I taught her architecture."

— Paolo - Pilot Mentor, Phase 1

2. Impact Tomorrow: Expansion & Scale

Planned for Year 1 (Q3) through Year 3

Our pilot is just the spark. Our strategy is designed for exponential growth, moving from direct service to a replicable global model.

Phase 2: The Acceleration (Year 1, Q3-Q4)

We are tripling our footprint to validate that our model works in diverse environments.

  • Expansion: Onboarding 4 additional schools and increasing our student reach by 100+ students.
  • Recruitment: Activating 20 additional Tech Pioneers, tapping into alumni networks of major tech firms.
  • The "Replication Toolkit": We are codifying our curriculum into a turnkey "box" that any community center or school can deploy.

Phase 3: The Global Standard (Year 2+)

Our ultimate goal is to change the educational landscape permanently.

  • National Campaign: Launching a nationwide recruitment drive to mobilize the retiring tech workforce.
  • International Pilot: Testing the model globally to prove that the language of code and connection transcends borders.
  • Systemic Change: Reducing the global teacher shortage gap (currently at 70M) by utilizing the 55+ demographic as a new, highly skilled teaching force.

Meet Our Mentors

Our mentors are industry veterans who are passing the torch to the next generation.

Dr. Paolo Narciso

Paolo is a social entrepreneur, technologist, and author who combines emerging technologies with social impact initiatives. He is a Co-Founder of PeopleCare.ai, a company focused on taking out the complexities of healthcare and putting control back in the patients’ hands by combining AI technology with a patient-first approach to develop community-centered healing solutions.

As the former Vice President at AARP Foundation, he led the development of innovative programs using AI, VR, and blockchain to address senior poverty. He is also the founder of Core Immersive Labs, a non-profit focused on creating opportunities for innovation in underserved communities, and serves as a Build Fellow at Stand Together Ventures Lab.

A published author with Springer Press, Paolo's recent works include practical guides on AI in education and strategic implementation of generative AI for organizations. As the Jack Welch Professor of Entrepreneurship and Management, he brings over three decades of experience to shaping future leaders.

Paolo holds a Doctorate from Creighton University and serves as a board advisor for multiple healthcare technology companies. His work consistently focuses on leveraging disruptive technologies to address societal challenges while promoting responsible innovation and sustainable social impact.

Dr. James McCoy

James has over 8 years of IT experience, is a builder and innovator in emerging technologies, such as AI/ML, blockchain, and Web3. He is currently the Chief Data Officer of the US Census Bureau where he applies his expertise in privacy enhancing technology and explainable AI to support the agency's data privacy and security initiatives.

Dr. McCoy received his Ph.D in Computer Science from Howard University.
